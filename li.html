Early Life & Education

A Journey of Resilience: Born in Beijing in 1976, Dr. Li immigrated to the U.S. at age 16. Despite the language barrier, she excelled academically in Parsippany, New Jersey.

Academic Excellence: She earned her undergraduate degree in physics from Princeton University.

Pioneering Research: She continued her studies at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), where she received her Ph.D. in electrical engineering, laying the groundwork for her future in AI.

Major Contributions

ImageNet: A Paradigm Shift

Dr. Li is the principal investigator of ImageNet, a groundbreaking project that created a massive, free database of over 14 million hand-annotated images. This provided the crucial, large-scale data needed to train deep learning models, sparking the revolution in computer vision.

Explore ImageNet

AI4ALL: Fostering Diversity

She co-founded AI4ALL, a non-profit aimed at increasing diversity and inclusion in AI. The organization runs educational programs for underrepresented high school students, inspiring the next generation of AI leaders.

Vision: Human-Centered AI

Beyond technical achievements, Dr. Li is a leading advocate for developing AI that is ethical, responsible, and beneficial for all of humanity.

"I imagine a world where AI is going to be our collaborator. It's going to be our partner. It's going to help us to be better humans."

Her work at the Stanford Institute for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HAI) focuses on studying, guiding, and developing AI in a way that serves the public interest.
